Riya read the clause twice: stability for optics. The words were clean until someone had to breathe under them.

The corridor’s clamp board showed green. The ward’s heat map showed a mosaic of yellows that meant “not yet panic.” She opened a micro‑window—twenty seconds—against procedure, inside the gray the policy left for discretion and blame.

“Log it temporary; monitor,” she said to no one, then to the record. Her hand hovered over the cancel. She did not cancel.

A janitor on the mezzanine tied a small knot and set the ledger under the rail. The compressor cooled a fraction. The fight she would have later would be about that fraction.

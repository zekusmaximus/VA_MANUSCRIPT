## V. Expansion Principles (Sensitive to Pacing)

### What to Add
‚úÖ **Sensory grounding**: Sights, sounds, smells, textures‚Äîmake scenes vivid
‚úÖ **Character interiority**: What characters think/feel in moments of decision
‚úÖ **Cultural specificity**: Details that make each community/city distinct
‚úÖ **Emotional beats**: Processing, reflection, human response to events
‚úÖ **Thematic resonance**: Moments that deepen core themes (growth, adaptation, breath)
‚úÖ **Transitional tissue**: Connective beats between major actions

### What NOT to Add
‚ùå **Plot detours**: No new subplots or tangents
‚ùå **Exposition dumps**: Don't explain what readers can infer
‚ùå **Repetitive content**: Avoid restating what's already clear
‚ùå **Padding**: No "empty calories" just to hit word count
‚ùå **Breaking rhythm**: Maintain existing scene flow and momentum

### Pacing Guidelines

**Slow down for**:
- Opening (Ch1): Establish world before disruption
- Threshold moments (Ch10): Weight to philosophical turning points
- Emotional beats (Ch12): Let resonance land
- Global scope (Ch9): Show scale of transformation

**Don't slow down**:
- Crisis sequences (Ch5 Arc-9 crisis)
- Vote day action (Ch6 Helios demo)
- Existing tight tactical scenes
- Scenes that already work at current length

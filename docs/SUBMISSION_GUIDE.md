### Standard Manuscript Formatting

```
- Font: 12pt Times New Roman or Courier
- Spacing: Double-spaced
- Margins: 1 inch all sides
- Header: Your Name / TITLE / Page Number (top right)
- First page: Title, by [Name], word count (top left)
- Chapter headers: Centered, begin 1/3 down page
- Paragraph indent: 0.5 inches (use tab, not spaces)
- One space after periods (industry standard)
- No extra line breaks between paragraphs
```

## 2. Query Letter Strategy

### Choosing Your Version

**Version 1 (Standard)** - Use if:
- You're new to querying
- Agent prefers traditional format
- You want the safest, most professional approach

**Version 2 (Hook-Forward)** - Use if:
- You want to stand out in slush pile
- Agent's MSWL mentions unique premises
- You're comfortable with bold opening

**Version 3 (Character-Driven)** - Use if:
- Agent represents emotional, character-focused SFF
- Agent's client list includes books with strong internal conflict
- You want to emphasize Amara's personal journey

### Personalization Is Critical

**Research each agent:**
1. Check their #MSWL (manuscript wish list)
2. Read their interviews/blog posts
3. Look at their recent sales (Publishers Marketplace)
4. Review their client list
5. Note their submission preferences

**Personalization examples:**
- "I'm querying you because you represented [TITLE] and mentioned loving infrastructure-as-character in your interview with [SOURCE]."
- "Your #MSWL request for 'hopepunk with teeth' describes THE VERDANT ACCORD perfectly."
- "I saw you're looking for optimistic SFF that doesn't shy from systemic critique, which resonates with my novel's core."

**Never send generic queries.** Agents can tell.

## 3. Comparable Titles (Comps)

### The Comp Formula

**Your book = [Comp A] meets [Comp B]**
Or: **Your book will appeal to readers who loved [Comp A] and [Comp B]**

2. **THE TERRAFORMERS** by Annalee Newitz (2023)

## 5. Querying Strategy

### Batch Querying

**Don't query everyone at once.** Test your materials first.

**Batch 1 (5-10 queries):**
- Send to Tier 2-3 agents (save dream agents)
- Wait 4-6 weeks
- Track response rate

**Evaluate results:**
- Multiple requests? Materials are strong - proceed
- Some requests? Materials are decent - maybe revise query
- No response? Revise query before next batch

**Batch 2 (10-15 queries):**
- Include revised query (if needed)
- Mix of Tier 2-3 agents
- Wait 4-6 weeks

**Batch 3+ (15-20 queries):**
- Include Tier 1 dream agents
- Send when confident in materials

### Timeline Expectations

- **Query response:** 2-8 weeks (some never respond)
- **Partial request:** Agent wants first 50 pages
- **Full request:** Agent wants full manuscript
- **Response to full:** 4-12 weeks (can be longer)
- **Offer:** When agent wants to represent you

**Industry norm:** Many agents don't respond to queries unless interested ("no response means no")

### Tracking Your Queries

Use a spreadsheet:

## 6. When You Get Requests

### Partial Request (First 50 pages)

**Response time:** Within 24-48 hours
1. Thank them professionally
2. Attach formatted pages as requested (usually PDF or Word)
3. Keep it brief - no need to re-pitch
4. Continue querying others

**Email template:**
```
Dear [Agent Name],

Thank you for your interest in THE VERDANT ACCORD. I've attached the first 50 pages as requested.

Looking forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### Full Request

**Response time:** Within 24-48 hours
1. Same professional response
2. Attach full manuscript (formatted)
3. Mention any competing interest (if you have other fulls out)
4. Continue querying

**If you have multiple fulls out:**
"Thank you for your request. I should mention that the full manuscript is also under consideration at [Agency Name], though I have not yet received an offer of representation."

### The Offer Call

**When an agent offers representation:**

1. **Thank them and ask for time:** "Thank you so much! May I have a week to consider?"
2. **Notify other agents with your full:** "I've received an offer of representation and wanted to let you know, as you have my full manuscript."
3. **Prepare questions for the call** (see below)
4. **Make your decision within agreed timeframe**

**Questions to ask:**
- What is your vision for this book?
- What revisions do you anticipate?
- What publishers would you submit to?
- What's your submission strategy and timeline?
- How do you communicate with clients?
- What are your commission rates? (Standard is 15% domestic, 20% foreign)
- Can I speak with some of your current clients?
- Do you have a foreign rights team/film agent?

## 7. Red Flags to Avoid

### Legitimate Agents Never:
- Charge reading fees
- Require editing services before representation
- Ask for money upfront for any reason
- Guarantee publication
- Pressure you to sign immediately

### Warning Signs:
- No recent sales listed
- Can't find them on QueryTracker or Publishers Marketplace
- Generic website with multiple businesses
- Poor grammar in communications
- Offer representation after only seeing query (not reading manuscript)

### Verify Legitimacy:
- Check Association of Authors' Representatives (AAR) membership
- Look up sales on Publishers Marketplace
- Search "[Agent Name] + scam" or "warning"
- Check Preditors & Editors
- Read QueryTracker comments

## 9. Rejection & Persistence

### Types of Rejections

**Form rejection:** Standard "not right for my list"
- Don't take personally
- Most common type
- Learn nothing from it

**Personal rejection:** Specific feedback
- Gold! Save and analyze
- Agent saw potential
- Use feedback to revise

**Request then rejection:** They read pages/full
- Still meaningful interest
- Get feedback if offered
- Could be subjective, not quality

### When to Revise

**Revise query if:**
- Zero requests after 20-30 queries
- Multiple agents say same thing about query
- You realize you didn't convey the story clearly

**Revise manuscript if:**
- Multiple agents cite same issue in pages
- You get requests but all reject after reading
- Trusted beta readers identify major problems

**Don't revise if:**
- Only a few rejections
- Feedback is contradictory
- It's purely subjective taste

### Keep Going

- **Industry average:** 50-100 queries before finding agent
- **Timeline:** 6-12 months is normal
- **Success stories exist:** Many debuts found agents after 100+ queries
- **Multiple books:** Many authors land agents on book 2 or 3

**"A rejection is just one person's opinion on one day."**
